                    <div class="label">Further-simple explanation</div>
                  </div>
                  <button class="button">
                    Authorization Accepted (AA) is when the customer's bank
                    verifies they have sufficient funds for the transaction. If
                    approved, the payment moves forward. This is the initial
                    step in the payment process. Presentment (PT) occurs after
                    the bank approves the transaction. The merchant shares the
                    transaction details with their bank, which collaborates with
                    the customer's bank to finalize the payment. This step
                    transfers the money from the customer's account to the
                    merchant's account. In simpler terms, AA is the first stage,
                    where the transaction gets approved, and PT is the second
                    stage, where the money gets transferred.
                  </button>
                  <div class="label-wrapper">
                    <div class="label">Scenarios</div>
                  </div>
                  <button class="button">
                    There are generally three scenarios that can occur after
                    theAuthorization Accepted (AA): 1.Successful Presentment
                    (PT): The merchant collects the funds by submitting the
                    transaction details to their bank, which works with your
                    bank to complete the payment. The transaction proceeds as
                    expected, and the amount is deducted from your account.
                    2.Authorization Reversal: If the merchant decides not to
                    collect the funds or cancels the transaction, they can
                    perform an authorization reversal or void the authorization.
                    In this case, the authorized amount is released back to your
                    available balance within a specified period (typically 3 to
                    7 days, depending on the merchant policies). 3.Authorization
                    Expiration: If the merchant does not initiate the
                    presentment within a certain time frame (usually between 7
                    to 30 days, depending on the policies of the banks
                    involved), the authorization expires. When this happens, the
                    funds that were held on your account will be released back
                    to your available balance automatically.
                  </button>
                  <div class="label-wrapper">
                    <div class="label">Chargeback</div>
                  </div>
                  <button class="button">
                    A chargeback request typically cannot be initiated while the
                    transaction is still in the Authorization Accepted (AA)
                    stage. It can only be requested once the transaction has
                    been fully processed, and the funds have been transferred
                    from your account to the merchant's account. In this case, I
                    strongly advise you to contact the merchant directly to
                    resolve the issue or request a cancellation. If the merchant
                    agrees to cancel the transaction, they can initiate an
                    authorization reversal or void the authorization, which will
                    release the held funds back to your available balance. In
                    case that the merchant cannot track this payment yet, I can
                    gladly provide you with the authorization code which helps
                    them to track and cancel the payment. If the transaction
                    proceeds to Presentment (PT) and is completed, and you still
                    have concerns or believe the transaction was
                    unauthorized,incorrect, or otherwise disputable, you can
                    then initiate a chargeback request through us, directly from
                    your N26 app or by contact us directly in chat.
                  </button>

    $(document).on("click", ".Tnav-button", function () {
    const TcontentId = $(this).text().replace(" ", "_").trim();

    // Hide all content divs
    $(".content-div").hide();
    $("#" + TcontentId).show();

    // Toggle active state for buttons
    $(".Tnav-button").removeClass("active");
    $(this).addClass("active");
  });
